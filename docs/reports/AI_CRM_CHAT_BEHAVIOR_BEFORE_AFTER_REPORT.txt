ChatZi AI CRM Chat Behavior Report
==================================

Date: 2026-06-26
Scope: AI chat behavior, CRM ticket flow, lead capture, duplicate ticket prevention


1. Summary
----------

We improved the bot behavior for purchase/support-style conversations by moving CRM understanding to an AI-first structured classifier.

The bot no longer relies on hardcoded customer phrases to understand purchase intent, booking intent, support intent, customer name, phone number, or requested product/service.

The system now uses AI structured JSON extraction from recent conversation context, while keeping only technical data-safety guardrails such as phone normalization and duplicate-ticket prevention.


2. Chat State Before
--------------------

The previous behavior was poor in the provided conversation:

- The customer asked about the company's services.
- The bot sometimes replied with generic identity text instead of useful business knowledge.
- The customer said they wanted to buy iPhone 15.
- The bot asked for name and phone.
- The customer provided name and phone multiple times.
- The bot did not reliably reuse the provided data.
- The bot asked again for the same name and phone.
- Ticket and lead creation were not reliable from the conversation flow.
- Duplicate ticket prevention was mostly tied to the same conversation, not the same phone number.

Example of the problem:

- Customer: "أسمي محمود عادل رقم الهاتف 01005840966"
- Bot later: "أحتاج إلى اسمك ورقم هاتفك"

This made the bot look as if it had no memory and no real CRM action.


3. Root Causes
--------------

The main causes were:

- CRM field extraction was not strong enough.
- Fast responder could run before CRM routing and intercept short business messages.
- The system did not reliably use recent conversation context when deciding whether all required CRM fields were already provided.
- Ticket deduplication did not fully protect against multiple open tickets for the same phone number.
- Earlier mitigation used phrase-based routing/parsing, which the product owner rejected because it felt like hardcoding.


4. What Was Changed
-------------------

4.1 AI-first CRM classifier

The CRM flow now depends on an AI structured classifier that returns strict JSON.

The classifier receives:

- Latest customer message.
- Recent conversation messages.
- Active CRM ticket flow state, if any.
- Required fields policy.
- Allowed ticket categories.
- Business settings and instructions.

It returns structured data such as:

- action
- category
- priority
- collectedFields.name
- collectedFields.phone
- collectedFields.issueDescription
- reason
- confidence


4.2 Recent conversation context

The workflow now loads recent messages from the conversation and passes them into the CRM classifier.

This allows the bot to understand a later message like "أكد طلب الشراء" or "تمام" based on earlier messages where the customer already mentioned the product, name, and phone.


4.3 CRM routing before fast responder

The AI CRM ticket flow now runs before the lightweight fast responder.

This prevents short business-critical messages from being treated as generic greetings or unclear messages.


4.4 Ticket creation behavior

When required fields are complete:

- The system reaches create_ticket.
- The ticket can be created or updated.
- The bot confirms naturally.
- The bot does not ask again for already collected fields.


4.5 Duplicate ticket prevention

The ticket service now checks for existing open tickets using:

- tenant
- open ticket status
- normalized customer phone
- contactId when available
- conversation fingerprint/category fallback

If an open ticket already exists for the same phone number, the system updates that ticket instead of creating a duplicate.


4.6 Lead deduplication

Lead creation/update now deduplicates using:

- normalized phone
- email
- contactId

This reduces repeated leads for the same customer across conversations or channels.


5. Hardcoded Logic Status
-------------------------

Current status: No hardcoded business or language routing logic remains in the AI chat/CRM understanding path.

Specifically:

- No hardcoded product names.
- No hardcoded prices.
- No hardcoded company services.
- No hardcoded customer purchase phrases such as "مهتم", "أريد شراء", or "أكد الطلب".
- No manual parser that depends on labels such as "الاسم", "رقم الهاتف", or "my name is".
- No phrase list for deciding sales, booking, support, or complaint intent.

CRM understanding is now handled by the AI structured classifier using conversation context.


6. What Still Exists and Why
----------------------------

The remaining non-AI logic is technical guardrail logic only, not business hardcoding:

- Phone normalization.
- Arabic/Persian digit normalization for phone numbers.
- Minimum phone length validation.
- Rejecting short product-like numbers such as "15" from "iPhone 15".
- Database-level duplicate protection for open tickets.
- Lead deduplication by phone/email/contact.

These rules are data-safety protections. They do not define business behavior, products, services, or customer intent.


7. Chat State After
-------------------

For the original-style conversation:

Customer:
"ماهي خدمات الشركة المتحدة"

Customer:
"اريد شراء ايفون 15"

Customer:
"الاسم / محمود عادل رقم الهاتف/ 01002540633"

Expected behavior now:

- The services question goes to knowledge/reply generation.
- The purchase message starts the CRM ticket flow.
- The requested product is captured as issueDescription.
- Name and phone are captured from conversation context.
- Once all required fields are complete, the flow reaches create_ticket.
- The bot does not ask again for name or phone.
- If an open ticket already exists for the same phone number, it is updated instead of duplicated.
- A lead is created or updated without duplication.
